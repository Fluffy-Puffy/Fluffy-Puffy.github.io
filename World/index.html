The World Happiness Index

Happiness is a positive emotion that is deeper than a good temporary mood.
Happiness is made up of at least three main components: emotional, social, and cognitive notions.
A sustainable happiness promotes greater physical and mental health, longer life, better work output, and supportive social connections.
The benefits of happiness are broad and important. Therefore, it cannot be ignored.
Happy people not only feel better but they function better in achieving the things which are value.

Does GDP per Capita Influence Happiness?

In 2020, Degutis, Urbonavicius, and Gaizutis conducted a study on the relationship between GDP and life satisfaction and discovered a link between a country's life satisfaction and GDP level. However, more research into other influencing factors is required.

The heatmap visualize the relationship between Happiest Score and GDP per Capita. The darker the colour, the more related relationships are recorded. The heatmap demonstrates a positive relationship between GDP per capita and Happiness Score. Based on the chart, we can conclude that there is a relationship between a country's happiness score and its GDP per capita. One of the highest counts recorded is GDP between 10.5 and 11 with a happiness score of 7 to 8.

Do Happy People Live Longer Lives?

A new study found people who are in a better mood are 35% less likely to die in the next five years.

The scatter plot captures the relationship between Life Expectancy and Happiness Score. The regression is provided to describe the relationship. The relationships are tested to countries all over the world and the regions are described by the colours. Life Expectancy and Happiness Score were discovered to have a positive relationship. According to the scatter plot, Sub-Saharan Africa has the weakest relationship between happiness and life expectancy. Western Europe has the strongest relationship between Life Expectancy and Happiness Score.

How Does Corruption Affect Happiness?

According to Transparency International's global corruption reports and the United Nations World Happiness Report, there is a very strong relationship between a low level of corruption and a greater happiness score.

The radial chart depicts regional perceptions of corruption. The size of the chart indicates the level of corruption; the larger it is, the higher the level of corruption in the country is. According to the graph, Central and Eastern Europe has the highest average perception of corruption at 0.8475, followed by Latin America and the Caribbean at 0.7943. North America and Australia and New Zealand have the lowest corruption perception, with an average of 0.4405.

What is the Happiest Country in the World?

The bump chart visualise the countries with the highest levels of happiness around the world. The colours represent the countries. The data ranges from 2015 to 2021.
According to the chart, 12 countries have been in and out of the world's top ten happiest countries. Australia and Canada were once viewed as one of the happiness countries. Austria and Luxembourg joined the ranks of the world's happiest countries in 2019 and 2020.

In 2015, Finland is ranked as number 6 of the world happiest country. Finland keeps making an improvement in 2016 and 2017. In 2018, Finland reached number as the happiest country in the world. Since then, Finland has recorded as the world's happiest country from 2018 to 2021.
In terms of the factors that influence happiness, Finland has a logged GDP per capita of 10.775 , a life expectancy of 72 years, and a perception of corruption of 0.186 in 2021. Based on the observations, there is a correlation between GDP, life expectancy, and corruption with level of happiness .
